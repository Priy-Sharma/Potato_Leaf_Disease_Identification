Information

Causes:

  • Early blight of potato is caused by the fungus, Alternaria solani.
  • Early blight is favored by warm temperatures and high humidity.
  • It is found on older leaves first.

Preventions:

  • Avoid overhead irrigation.
  • Do not dig tubers until they are fully mature in order to prevent damage.
  • Do not use a field for potatoes that was used for potatoes or tomatoes the previous year.

Causes:

  • Late blight of potato is a serious disease caused by Phytophthora infestans.
  • It is favored by cool, moist weather and can kill plants within two weeks if conditions are right.
  • Late blight is the worst potato disease.

Preventions:

  • Use disease-free seed potatoes.
  • Keep cull/compost piles away from potato growing areas.
  • Keep tubers covered with soil throughout the season to prevent tuber infection.
  • Kill vines completely before harvest to avoid inoculation of the tubers during harvest.
